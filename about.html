Our Story

A community defined by its members.

We're the first women's chess club in England — and we're just getting started.

Who we are.

London Women Chess Club is a non-profit Community Interest Company (CIC) dedicated to providing a welcoming, safe space for all women and girls to learn and enjoy chess. We were founded on a simple belief: chess is for everyone, and the board feels better when everyone's invited.

As England's first women's chess club, we've grown into a vibrant community that spans every age, background and skill level. From total beginners discovering how the knight moves, to competitive players representing us in leagues, our members are what make us who we are.

What we stand for.

Our work is shaped by a few simple values:

  • Inclusivity. All women and girls are welcome, regardless of age, background, experience or ability.
  • Safe play. We hold ourselves to a high standard of safeguarding and community care.
  • Growth. Wherever you are on your chess journey, we want to help you get further.
  • Community. The club is built on real friendships and positive interactions — not just games.

Recognition.

We're proud to have been recognised by the English Chess Federation as an award-winning club for women in chess. We're supported by the ECF, ECF Grassroots, and the Francis Crick Institute — partnerships that help us run free sessions, host events, and grow.

Our People

Built by members, for members.

The club is truly defined by its members. We value each person's diverse talents and contributions — whether that's running tactics workshops, organising socials, mentoring new players, or simply showing up every Sunday with a smile.

We're a volunteer-led organisation with in-house coaches and senior players who give their time to help others learn. If you'd like to get involved beyond playing, there's almost certainly a way to contribute.
